Section=Legal Rights of Employees

Stem=An unlawful search and seizure will invalidate a subsequent conviction based on other evidence.

Options=True | False

CorrectResponse=1

Explanation=Under the exclusionary rule in effect in all federal and state courts, evidence seized in violation of the Fourth Amendment will be suppressed; that is. it may not be used against the accused. An unlawful search and seizure, however, does not preclude prosecution or invalidate a subsequent conviction based on other evidence.See page(s) 2.431 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
