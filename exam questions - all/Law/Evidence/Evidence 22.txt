Section=Evidence

Stem=A chain of custody should be maintained for every piece of physical evidence and all electronically stored information (ESI) evidence gathered and analyzed in an investigation.

Options=True | False

CorrectResponse=0

Explanation=Chain of custody issues affect physical evidence as well as electronically stored information. To prove the integrity of the evidence was maintained, a chain of custody must be established. Since ESI evidence can be easily altered or destroyed, chain of custody documentation is especially crucial with investigations concerning ESI.See page(s) 2.715-2.716 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
