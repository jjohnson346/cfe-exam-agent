Section=Evidence

Stem=Anything that is perceptible by the five senses legally presented at trial to prove a contention and induce a belief in the mind of the jury is called:

Options=Testimony | Point of Law | Evidence | None of the above

CorrectResponse=2

Explanation=Black's Law Dictionary defines evidence as: "Anything perceivable by the five senses, and any proof, such as testimony of witnesses, records, documents, facts, data, or tangible objects legally presented at trial to prove a contention and induce a belief in the minds of a jury." See page(s) 2.701 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
