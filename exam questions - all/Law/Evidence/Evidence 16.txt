Section=Evidence

Stem=Evidence which proves or disproves facts by inference is called direct evidence.

Options=True | False

CorrectResponse=1

Explanation=Direct evidence includes testimony that tends to prove or disprove a fact in issue directly, such as eyewitness testimony or a confession. Circumstantial evidence is evidence that tends to prove or disprove facts in issue indirectly, by inference.See page(s) 2.702 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
