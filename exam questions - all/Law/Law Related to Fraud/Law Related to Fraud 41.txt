Explanation=The Foreign Corrupt Practices Act of 1977 prohibits certain publicly held companies from making corrupt payments to foreign officials or political organizations. Other amendments to the Act make it illegal for any U.S. citizen to make such payments. The statute was the result of disclosures from the Watergate investigations of corporate grease payments to foreigners to obtain business overseas.See page(s) 2.249 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
