Section=Criminal Prosecutions for Fraud

Stem=Both individuals and businesses have a Fifth Amendment right not to be compelled to testify against themselves.

Options=True | False

CorrectResponse=1

Explanation=The Fifth Amendment provides that no person "shall... be compelled in any criminal case to be a witness against himself..." The Fifth Amendment applies:•	In criminal and civil cases•	To compelled "testimonial statements": Nontestimonial conduct, such as compelling a suspect to appear in a line-up, give handwriting samples, or submit to blood or alcohol tests, is not barred by the Fifth Amendment•	Only to individuals. Business entities, including sole proprietorships and other organizations, do not have a Fifth Amendment privilege.See page(s) 2.503 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
