Section=Covert Examinations

Stem=If a confidential informant refuses to reveal any information about a suspected offense until promised confidentiality, what steps should the examiner take?

Options=Promise the informant that his name will be kept out of the examination if possible | Promise the informant confidentiality | Assign the informant a code name and never use his real name | Any of the above

CorrectResponse=0

Explanation=Fraud examiners are not in a position to offer unqualified confidentiality. Therefore, any promises made to an informant or source should be qualified, such as by telling the informant that all efforts v/ill be made to ensure that his name is not revealed.See page(s) 3.318 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
