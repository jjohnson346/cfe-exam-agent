Section=Interview Theory and Application

Stem=Which of the following witnesses should be interviewed by surprise?

Options=Friendly witnesses | Neutral witnesses | Volatile witnesses | All of the above

CorrectResponse=2

Explanation=Surprise should be employed in any interview that is considered potentially volatile In many instances, the potentially volatile respondent is unaware that he is going to be questioned, and will therefore be offguard. If the interview is not conducted by surprise, the interviewer runs the risk of the respondent not showing up. showing up with a witness, or being present with counsel.See page(s) 3.233 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
