Section=Criminal Prosecutions for Fraud

Stem=The Supreme Court has held that police must have probable cause to briefly detain and question a person.

Options=True | False

CorrectResponse=1

Explanation=In Terry vs. Ohio. 392 U.S. 1 (1968). the Supreme Court held that police may briefly detain and question a person for investigative purposes if there are specific "articulable" reasons to do so The decision imposes a lesser standard than probable cause.See page(s) 2.503 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
