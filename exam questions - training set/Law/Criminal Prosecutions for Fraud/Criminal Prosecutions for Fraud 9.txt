Section=Criminal Prosecutions for Fraud

Stem=Under the Federal Rules of Criminal Procedure, both the prosecution and defense are permitted closing arguments after the evidence has been presented. In what order are closing arguments made?

Options=Defense, prosecution, defense | The order is at the discretion of the judge | Prosecution, defense, prosecution | None of the above

CorrectResponse=2

Explanation=In criminal trials, the prosecution argues first, the defense follows, and the prosecution has the opportunity for final rebuttal.See page(s) 2.522 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
