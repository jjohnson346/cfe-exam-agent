Section=Criminal Prosecutions for Fraud

Stem=Which Constitutional Amendment covers search warrants?

Options=First Amendment | Fourth Amendment | Fifth Amendment | Sixth Amendment

CorrectResponse=1

Explanation=The Fourth Amendment covers search warrants. It states: The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizure, shall not be violated, and no warrants shall issue, but upon probable cause, supported by oath or affirmation, and particularly describing the place to be searched, and the persons or things to be seized."See page(s) 2.501 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
