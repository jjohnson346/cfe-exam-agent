Section=Sources of Information

Stem=The last six digits of a Social Security number are:

Options=Indicators of the city of issue | Indicators of the state of issue | Individual identifiers | None of the above

CorrectResponse=2

Explanation=There are nine digits in a Social Security number. The first three digits reflect the state of issue; the last six digits are individual identifiers.See page(s) 3.434 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
