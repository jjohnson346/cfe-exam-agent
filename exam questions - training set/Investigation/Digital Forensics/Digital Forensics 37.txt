Explanation=Evidence may also be found in information generated by the operating systems. This type of information is important because it can identify that a certain activity has taken place, and, in most cases, the user is not aware that it is being written. Some common examples of computer-generated data available for examination include, metadata, registry, paging file, event logs, LNK files, Internet activity, temporary data, deleted data, and printer-spool files.See page(s) 3.725 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
