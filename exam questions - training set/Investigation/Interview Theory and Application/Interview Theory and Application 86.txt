Section=Interview Theory and Application

Stem=When people engage in conversations, including interviews, they respond automatically with expressions that have little real meaning, such as "Good morning," and "How are you today?" These kinds of expressions are called:

Options=Therapeutic | Expressionistic | Ritualistic | None of the above

CorrectResponse=2

Explanation=Some aspects of conversation are ritualistic; that is, they merely are a form of verbal behavior that has no real significance other than to provide security in interpersonal relations. Examples include "Good morning," and "How are you today?" In interviewing, we must learn to detect ritualistic answers by the respondent, and avoid giving them ourselves. The interviewer must be aware of the danger of engaging in ritualistic conversation and then confusing the results with valid information.See page(s) 3.206 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
