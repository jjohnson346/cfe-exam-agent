Stem=When conducting an interview of a volatile witness, it is proper procedure to:

Options=Approach the witness without an appointment | Conduct the interview in a public place | Always record the interview | All of the above

CorrectResponse=0

Explanation=Surprise should be employed in any interview that is considered potentially volatile In many instances, potentially volatile respondents are unaware that they are going to be questioned, and will therefore be offguard. If the interview is not conducted by surprise, the interviewer runs the risk of the respondent not showing up. showing up with a witness, or being present with counsel.See page(s) 3.233 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
