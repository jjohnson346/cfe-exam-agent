Section=Check and Credit Card Fraud

Stem=Able, a Certified Fraud Examiner, is looking at a check that he suspects is forged. It appears that the forger may have used correction fluid to white out the name of the payee. Able decides that he needs to call in a forensic document specialist. The tool that the specialist v/ill most likely use to reveal the use of correction fluid is a(n):

Options=Biometric fingerprint identifier | Electrostatic detection apparatus | Video spectral comparator | Correction fluid analyzer

CorrectResponse=2

Explanation=A video spectral comparator is used by forensic examiners to test questionable documents. The document is examined for photocopying and correction fluid/tape under a controlled light source. Lettering added to a document can be detected with this device.See page(s) 1.1013 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
