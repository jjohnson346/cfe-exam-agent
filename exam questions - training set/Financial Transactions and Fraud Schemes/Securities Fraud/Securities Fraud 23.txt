Section=Securities Fraud

Stem=The Securities Exchange Act of 1934 prohibits which of the following in connection with the purchase or sale of securities?

Options=Making any untrue statement of fact | Omitting a material fact | Employing any scheme to defraud | All of the above

CorrectResponse=3

Explanation=The 1934 Act contains comprehensive anti-fraud provisions. Rule 10b-5, for example, states that: "It shall be unlawful for any person, directly or indirectly, by the use of any means or instrumentality of interstate commerce, or of the mails, or of any facility of any national securities exchange, (1) to employ any device, scheme or artifice to defraud, (2) to make any untrue statement of a material fact or to omit to state a material fact necessary in order to make the statements made, in the light of the circumstances under which they were made, not misleading, or (3) to engage in any act, practice, or course of business which operates or would operate as a fraud or deceit upon any person, in connection with the purchase or sale of any security."See page(s) 1.1502-1.1503 in the Fraud Examiner's Manual
