Explanation=In the case SEC v. Howey Co., 328 U.S. 293, 299 (1946), a company was selling subdivided sections of a Florida orange orchard that were to be managed by a subsidiary company. In its opinion in the case, the U.S. Supreme Court outlined a four-element test to determine if an investment contract was present.
